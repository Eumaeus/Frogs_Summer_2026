**Author and name(s). Who named it? Why not "Dionysus and the Underworld" or "The Contest of Poets?**

Aristophanes wrote *Frogs* (ΒΑΤΡΑΧΟΙ), An example of Greek Old Comedy featuring the main characters, Dionysus (ΔΙΟΝΥΣΟΣ) and his slave (ΞΑΝΟΙΑΣ). Aristophanes named his play *Frogs* to appeal to an audience composed of countryfolk. Stanford wrote that the population in the countryside of Attica were "those less self-assured people further back, with badly cut clothes and awkward gestures." [^1]: Aristophanes. *Frogs.* Commentary by W.B Stanford, xiii. The less cultured were seated behind the "cityfolk", rich aristocrats and politicians that lived within Attica proper,they were "further back" in the audience when plays were performed at festivals. Aristophanes needed to entertain the audience when plays were performed at festivals. Aristophanes needed to entertain the whole audience because their applause directly affected his chances of at winning the contest. Plays with titles like *Frogs* or *Birds* allowed Aristophanes a direct connection to the countryside population and their applause.[^2]: Aristophanes. *Frogs.* x-xiii. 

**What is "comedy" in a Greek setting?**. 

Comedy is real life in Classical Greece and in the modern world. The Oxford Classical Dictionary states Aristophanes is Old Comedy which inspired New Comedy. The goal of comedy was to reimagine mythology into the modern standards of 405 BCE timeframe which in turn reflected everyday life. Costumes were employed which were often phallic along gestures and satire. [^3]: Horneblower and Spawforth. *Oxford Classical Dictionary.* 368-370. A Chorus and a group of stock characters are featured usually in costumes and often hidden in the scenery like a frog or bird would be naturally to inhabit. [^4]: Pickard-Cambridge. *The Dramatic Festivals of Athens.* 238.  

**Where and when performed.**

The Lenaean Festival boasted a competition for playwrights. Aristophanes *Frogs* was first performed during the festival. The calendar dates for the Lenaean Festival are uncertain but have proven to be either at the end of January or the beginning of February. [^5]: Aristophanes. *Frogs.* x-xi.   

**Political/historical context**

Historically speaking, *Frogs* was first performed in 405 BCE. The Athenians had been at war (The Peloponnesian War) for more than 25 years. The Athenians were defeated six months after the first production of the *Frogs* at the Lenaean Festial. The countryside had essentially been stripped of agriculture and was in economic peril surviving on imported food shipped on variable seas. Not an easy time to be alive, disease, and overcrowding in the city or starvation in the country. Stanford explains that ethics were disputed, religions doubted, and the arts like music, fashion, poetry, and plays were changed by the literary deaths of Euripides and Sophocles in the last year. It was the end of an era, and change was abounding in Attica. [^6]: Aristophanes. *Frogs.* xiv-xi. 

Politically, Attica was in the middle of a fued between the democratic and oligarchical parties. Political privileges were revoked and the debate about ending the war was a hot topic heightening their economic troubles causing polarization and strife within their heirarchal system. Optimism was low among the population due to the war and its ramifications painting politicians in poor standing. [^7]: Aristophanes. *Frogs.* xvi.
